Our Mission

American Development for Africa is in support of the diversity initiative for the United States, and is consistent with the commitment to using free market and fair trade solutions to raise the living standard for African Americans at home, but further, to establish, promote, and nurture American influence among emergent post colonial democracies within the African continent..

Energy

Offshore drilling operation Whereas African nations are rife with abundant sources of energy, the United States is world renowned for its industrial ability to capture, extract and harness it. ADA has partnerships with world leaders in energy producing technologies who are eager to establish African markets for the technologies that power America. From nuclear power plants to drilling rigs and refineries, ADA is the premiere resource for Africa to exploit American efficiency and quality of design. As African and Caribbean governments continue to engage in the privatization of their energy sectors, ADA’s body of investors are equally wiling to finance Africa’s modernization and expansion of dependable power. Prospects in bio-fuels, hydroelectricity, natural gas and other alternative energy sources also await investment.

Agriculture

The 21st Century is destined to usher in a new era of African advancement across the gamut of human endeavors; like all other periods of renaissance and expansion of human capital, there is none other as necessary component than nutrition. American Development for Africa has formed in order to facilitate speedy and effective transitions to agricultural technologies that eliminate malnutrition and food shortages; ultimately, generating substantial profits for nations who acquire American machinery.

Beyond

In line with the United Nations development goal number 17: “Revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development”, ADA was established to facilitate enhanced partnership between the United States private sector and Countries in the African region. While official development assistance from US Government agencies and financial intervention from multilateral development financial instutions are still essential, sustainable development hinges on the ability of African countries to develop local capacity to drive economic growth from within. ADA intends to tap into our vast network of American corporate, institutional and private investors to increase foreign direct investment (FDI), as well as technical collaboration and knowledge exchange programs geared towards contributing to sustainable economic growth within the African region. Our Approach is to connect American businesses and investors with opportunities in Africa with a focus on the following sectors:

  • Energy
  • Mining
  • Infrastracture
  • Health
  • Agriculture
  • Telecommunication
  • PPP

Mining

Like energy and agriculture, the extraction of mineral wealth is best accomplished by leveraging the technological and industrial might of the United States. This is especially relevant for the most mineral rich continent on planet Earth, and American industrialists have taken notice..

ADA partners with American Mining Industry for Africa ADA investors and industrialists alike have a keen interest in establishing “win-win” partnerships with state and private concerns. Nations with an interest in increasing the net yield of their mineral extraction operations while minimizing the impact on the environment are the preferred customer for American mining manufacturers. From technologies used in geological surveys, to excavators, to sorting and refining, ADA partnerships with American industry leaders are strategically developed such that mining operations are accurate, efficient, and deliver maximum tonnage of commodities to market at greater speeds than are available from US competitors. .

About

American Development for Africa Much of Sub-Saharan Africa over the last two decades, has established impressive economic growth. This can be evidenced by several factors, including the IMF’s upgrading of several Countries in the region, such as Botswana, Ghana, Kenya, Mozambique, Nigeria, Tanzania, Uganda and Zambia to the rank of “emerging markets”. With an exponentially expanding middle class, high economic growth rates, stable political environment in most Countries, and a sustained, robust youth population, the African continent has a unique opportunity to overcome its age-old developmental challenges. .
